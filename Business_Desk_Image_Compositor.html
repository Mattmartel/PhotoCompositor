USER GUIDE
Workflow
  1. Pick a template — choose 1up (full-bleed + portrait overlay) or 2up–5up multi-panel layouts.
  2. Drop a background image into the main slot. For 1up, this fills the entire 1920×1080 frame.
  3. Add a portrait overlay (1up only) — load a photo into the Portrait overlay slot, then click Remove BG to cut out the subject. The portrait floats over the background.
  4. Adjust position — click either slot to activate crop controls. Drag on the preview or use sliders to reposition.
  5. Apply treatment — choose a colour wash or B&W grade. The portrait overlay is always redrawn in full colour on top.
  6. Export — set quality and click Download JPG.
1up — Full-bleed + Portrait
The 1up template fills the entire canvas with a single background image. A second image — the portrait overlay — is composited on top with its background removed.
The portrait scales to fit the canvas height by default. Use the Zoom and X/Y sliders (or drag on the canvas) to position it left, right, or anywhere you like.
Tip: combine a B&W or colour-washed background with a full-colour portrait for maximum editorial impact. The portrait is always redrawn in full colour above any treatment.
Crop & Position
Click a slot card to activate its crop controls. Controls apply only to that slot.
Background slot: Zoom and X/Y offset pan within the panel frame. Drag on the preview or use the d-pad for coarse moves.
Portrait overlay: Zoom scales the cut-out subject. X/Y offsets move it across the canvas. Drag on the preview to reposition.
Background Removal
The Remove BG button runs an on-device AI model — no server, no upload. First use downloads the model (~40 MB); subsequent uses are instant.
Once removed, only the subject is rendered. It can then be zoomed and repositioned freely across the full canvas.
Tip: for best results use a source photo where the subject is well-lit against a plain or contrasting background. Re-click Remove BG to toggle off.
Colour Treatment
None — full colour, no overlay.
B&W — desaturated, natural contrast.
B&W+ — desaturated with boosted contrast. Best for dramatic single-subject images.
Colour washes — a tinted gradient across the composite. Choose a direction and wash colour. The portrait overlay is always redrawn in full colour above the treatment.
Multi-panel Layouts
The 2up–5up layouts divide the canvas into equal vertical strips. 2×2 creates four squares. 1+2 and 2+1 are hero layouts with one large panel and two stacked panels.
In multi-panel mode the portrait overlay is not available — use the ✂ BG button on any individual slot to cut out a subject and let it bleed across panel boundaries instead.
